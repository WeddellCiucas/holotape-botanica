TULIP
──────────────────────────── 


⸙ It's a bulbous perennial plant that can be found in ruined gardens and meadow-like terrain. 
⸙ The leaves are strap-shaped, and the flowers are large and colorful; usually red, yellow, pink and white, but also black, dual-colored or speckled.
⸙ The flowers emerge in spring, followed by capsule fruits with flat seeds. 
⸙ Native to steppes and temperate regions. Thrives in moderate climates.
⸙ The flowers can be crafted in a poultice and used topically to soothe insect bites, bee stings, scratches, skin irritations and minor burns. 
⸙ The sap can act as a diuretic and mild antiseptic. 
⸙ The bulbs, stems and leaves are toxic, especially if consumed raw. 
⸙ High allergy risks in certain individuals.
