PINYON PINE
──────────────────────────── 


⸙ It's a small, slow-growing conifer native to arid mountain regions.
⸙ It grows typically between 10-20 ft with gnarled trunks and rounded crowns.
⸙ The needles grow in pairs, about 2-3 inches long, and the cones are small and woody. They mature in 2 years and contain edible seeds called pinon nuts.
⸙ The bark is gray-brown and scaly, often resinous, and the age of this tree can even exceed 500 years.
⸙ The nuts are rich in protein, with healthy fats and essential minerals like magnesium and zinc.
⸙ They were traditionally used by tribals for respiratory ailments and wound healing.
⸙ The resin and sap can be applied as antiseptic.⸙
